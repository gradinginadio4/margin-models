Margin Models in Equipment Distribution

Equipment distribution has seen margin pressure in recent years due to global competition and direct manufacturer sales. Some Belgian companies have stabilized their position by evolving their business model beyond pure product resale.

Three Approaches We've Observed

Service Revenue Model
Bundling maintenance contracts, technical support, and ongoing service with equipment sales. Creates recurring revenue and deeper client relationships.
Example: Antwerp distributor now generates 30% of revenue from service contracts, providing steadier annual flow.
Subscription Model
Offering complete kitchen solutions as monthly subscriptions rather than capital purchases. Includes equipment, maintenance, and periodic upgrades.
Example: Ghent integrator shifted to "kitchen-as-a-service," improving cash flow predictability and customer retention.
Consulting Model
Positioning as kitchen design consultants and project managers, not just equipment suppliers. Fees for expertise separate from product margins.
Example: Brussels firm added design services, capturing projects earlier in planning cycle and reducing price competition.
